Exporting Chinese Internet Literature 

EXPLORATORY RESEARCH | BUSINESS STRATEGY | MARKET RESEARCH

1.  THE CHALLENGE

Since 2004, Chinese internet literature has started to go to the world. With Southeast Asia as the first station, it went through the exploration period of exporting popular single books, to the accumulation period of establishing translation websites in Europe and the United States, and then to the development period of  the strategic cooperation between China and foreign countries. 2.  RESEARCH PROCESS

User Side

To understand overseas internet literature readers, we conducted a survey on their reading behaviors and preferences.

  • The most beloved online literature category was romance novel
  • More than half read on the web 
  • Only 10% had read Chinese internet literature before

                                                                                                  The fact that most overseas novel enthusiasts were still stuck on the PC end and had not yet developed the reading habits on the mobile end was consistent with the market research data

Business/Market side 

We researched the Chinese internet literature export market by investigating the current players from the lens of product formats, content strategy, site visits, user acquisition channels, user engagement/retention, personas, business modes, financing status,etc.

  • The domestic novel content export platform developed slowly. There were few leading players and the market was at Blue Ocean stage.


  • At this stage, the main business model (advertising, tipping, crowdfunding and subscription) was immature, which was maintly reflected in losses of leading players. 

Technical Side

As the core barrier of exporting Chinese internet literature, AI translation technology laid the foundation of the industry development.

Readability test

In order to verify whether the underlying AI translation technology was mature enough for application at this stage. We conducted an online test to investigate the intelligibility of the novel content translated by existing translation platforms and collected 118 responses from overseas users. The highest rated platform received an average of 83 readability score out of 100, proving that the current underlying technology laid the cornerstone of AI translation and it was worth further exploration.

Interviews with technical experts

Next, we consulted technical experts in NLP and learnt more details about the constraints and advantages of the use of AI technology in translation: compared with human translation, ideally AI technology can speed up the translation process by 2-3 times, but proofreading is still needed to ensure the quality of translation.

Other Considerations

To facilitate the business decision making, we also projected the cost, planned resource allocation and drafted team building plans.

3.  INSIGHTS AND BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS

Through the preliminary research, we found that the commercial monetization model of Chinese internet literature products was not mature both at home and abroad, thus this type of content exporting product belongs to traffic-type (best for the user acquisition) content products. We also pointed out pain points from the following perspectives:

  •  Content Platforms: Immature Business model; Expensive translation cost; Slow update speed; Quality control.
  •  Authors: Low income (the cost mainly goes to translation); Difficult rights protection.
  •   Users:Cultural barriers; Translation quality; Long waiting time. 

As such, our team recommended to consider development direction as a content distribution platform, where AI translation technology will be applied to address the pain points of high translation cost and long update time.
