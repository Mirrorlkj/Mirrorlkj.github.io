Building a Customer-Facing Beauty Platform

EXPLORATORY UX RESEARCH | PRODUCT STRATEGY | MARKET RESEARCH

1.  THE CHALLENGE

Consumers always get lost in picking a right product from increasing offerings of beauty and personal care products in the market. Having felt this struggle before, our team started with the goal of enhancing shopping experience by building a customer-facing beauty platform to provide personalized recommendations and help guide consumer purchasing decisions. To build a platform that best solves users’ needs and pains, I conducted UX research to better understand current product researching and shopping habits of consumers and identified opportunities to improve that experience.

My Role

User Researcher

Objective

Answer whether and how we can help consumers find beauty and personal care products that best suit them.

Teams

A Start-up Team of 11 ,

Product Owner

Product Manager

UX Designer, etc.

2.  RESEARCH PROCESS

Phase 1 User Interview + Discount Usability Test 

In order to learn how users complete a beauty product purchasing cycle, and what they feel works well, where they think there are issues, and how they think things could be improved via tools/platforms, we conducted 20 user interviews mixed with discount usability tests on users across age groups, genders and socioeconomic backgrounds.

In user interviews, we broke down questions into 4 key themes:

1) Gather key background information: Socio-economic-geographical profiles and user engagement levels with beauty products

2) Define consumer behavioral norms and pain points with regards to beauty products shopping experience - how does it differ by online vs offline, personal care vs cosmetics, web page vs mobile, etc.

3) Discount Usability Test: Understand how they actually navigate within and across platforms to go from discovery to purchase

We asked users to use a competitor app to complete a task: "Say that you are interested in purchasing a new eye cream, please explain to me how you would go about it?", and observed how they completed it. While users walking us through the product selection process, we were able to examine:
     •  their key focus
     • navigation norms once entering user experience flow- i.e. user stayed in home page for a long time, user immediately checked              out all tabs, user was stuck trying to complete a task in a certain screen
     •  what confuses them
     •  what ticks their interest
     •  what/how they expect app to do in fulfilling their key needs
     •  what are important features in app that were overlooked (indicating no strong enough call to action in design)
     •  any UI aesthetics (design, color, size, button etc.) preferences

4) Conclusive Questions on broad wants & needs to improve beauty shopping experience

Phase 2 User Stories & Personas 

After synthesizing and analyzing interview data together with the product manager and the UX designer, we mapped our insights and findings into user stories and personas.

Examples of user stories:

“ As a skincare product user, I would like to look at the reviews/evaluations from the professional individuals   (doctors, dermatologists) with references, so that I will feel the info more credible.”

“As a cautious buyer, I would like to see only real, unsponsored reviews, so that I can obtain legitimate and truthful opinions on beauty products”

“As a mobile app shopper , I would like to compare multiple products without exiting & re-opening, so that I more easily see all desired items (i.e., replicate opening multiple tabs on web page).” 

Phase 3 Survey

Based on the shopping behaviors and pain points that recorded in the interviews, a short survey was constructed to assess the scale of found patterns and the magnitude of encountered issues. In addition, in order to speak to emerging doubts that tech team held in developing features and algorithms, I also designed survey questions interrogating consumer sentiments and behaviors towards product recommendation in general . 

After developing the survey, we recruited participants based on the personas constructed in the phase 2 to target at potential users. As a result, 396 valid responses were summarized into the following insights:

1)  Professional, personalized, effective and reliable product recommendation is what consumers are mainly looking for.

      •  Consumers form a circle of trust centered at personal experience, extending from acquaintances to bloggers and medical experts.
      •  RED is one of the most trusted platforms by users, and other popular platforms include Bilibili, Zhihu and Weibo.

2)  Personal care/Skincare products have a larger potential market than cosmetics.

3)  Reasons of recommendations should be focused mainly on the product effect, while considering brands, ingredients and prices.
      •  For beauty products, price > ingredient = brand
      •  For personal care products, ingredient = price > price 

Phase 4 Competitive Analysis 

Before getting into details of the development, we wanted to figure out what was available in the market and thus conducted a competitive analysis, which examined UIs, user flows, features, differentiators and user feedback of global and local competitors. 

3.  Research Findings & Recommendations

I communicated research results to the whole team through presentations and reports, which helped prioritize unmet user needs. Here are some examples:

Opportunity 1: How might we help users find products they trust?

Research Finding: Users trust acquaintances and professionals more 

Because of prevailing deceptive advertisements and not readily recognized sponsored contents, consumers grow cautious about the reviews and recommendations on beauty products. Therefore, they believe their own experience and people around them because they think that they won’t lie to them; they believe in health professionals because they think experts are more scientific and objective.

Recommended Solution：

While making product recommendations, 1) motivate consumers by creating a sense of community (e.g. presenting concrete data: “85% of people with your skin type like this product.”) or 2) leverage ingredients breakdown and medical evidences to craft reasoning (e.g. tell users how an ingredient will help them address their skin issues: “we recommend this soap because it contains Pyrithione Zinc, which will help you fight both bacteria and fungus.”)

Opportunity 2: How might we create personalized recommendation experience for users?

Research Finding: Users have different product research habits 

As revealed in personas, users can be grouped into 3 categories, those who prefer to do in-depth research about products by themselves, those who rely more on professionals or public opinions in decision making, and those who barely research. Different users have different levels of needs in understanding products being recommended, Therefore, we need to consider how to respond to their expectations respectively.

Recommended Solution：

In on boarding process, collect information about users’ skin conditions and their preferences in for products/brands to match their confirmation bias. With such data, we will be able to customize the home page to show product recommendations from a few dimensions that are of interests to specific group of users. This would potentially elevate user engagement at the first step.

4.  Reflections

Impact is determined from the beginning

You never know what you can learn from users before you see the research results, it’s true. But it’s also true that something is and needs to be predetermined — research objectives. In choosing research methods, designing research and framing questions, we periodically go back to goals set for the current and entire research. Research objectives determine what questions we will be answering and what design or business problems we can solve by research. A clear and well thought research objective could not only prevent us from getting lost in massive amounts of data, but also allow us to impact in a meaningful way. 

It’s our research!

UX research is teamwork. I collaborated with other team members on collecting and analyzing user data and communicated research results and insights to support the development of a user-centered product.

“The benefits of collaboration range from eliciting more design ideas and validating findings to removing potential blind spots and reducing workload”                                                                                                       ——  NN/g "How UX Professionals Collaborate on Deliverables"

5.  Next Steps

After the initial exploration, we got a general understanding of our potential users and a feeling of what our product will be like. Next steps of UX research will be implementing more generative research methods. I planned on refining app user flows by identifying user journeys and critical tasks, crafting strong recommendation reasoning via insights from behavioral experiments, and determining features through A/B testings.
